General Questions & Answers


System

What is Unmasked?

Unmasked is a facial recognition and mask detection system that will detect people within the school system who are not wearing a mask. There are four key components of this system: Facial Detection Model, Facial Recognition Model, a database, and an UI interface.

How does the system work?

The Facial Detection Model is a pre-trained model that detects the existence of a single face. The Facial Recognition Model identifies students by taking a screenshot of an individual and compares the images that we have stored in our database. The model will document the offense by storing the image at the time under that individual's folder. When the system is triggered an administrator is notified and is expected to manually review each automatic trigger, for false positives.

Who is the primary target for this software?

Schools and communities who wish to protect themselves through the use of masks.

What if I am no longer a student? Is my information still in the database?

You will need to contact someone on our team and let them know you are no longer a student. They will make sure you are removed from the system.
